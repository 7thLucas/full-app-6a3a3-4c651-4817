# Product Overview

## Product Identity

**Name:** Driftoria
**Category:** AI Character & Narrative App — visual companion chat + autonomous living stories
**Benchmark:** Emochi (visual 1:1 AI character chat). Driftoria matches its visual,
instant, parasocial chat experience **and** adds autonomous narrative progression on top.
**Phase:** Both modes built (MVP previewable) — Story Mode and Chat Mode live over one
backend. See [dual-mode-proposal.md](./dual-mode-proposal.md).

---

## Two Modes (Core Strategy)

Driftoria ships **two doors over one backend**. Same character + LLM + autonomous
engine spine; two distinct experiences.

| Mode | Feel | POV | For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Chat Mode** (Emochi-class, new) | parasocial "Companion" | 1st-person, talk *to* the character | mass market — instant, visual, intimate |
| **Story Mode** (current, keep) | literary "Director" | 3rd-person living world | fiction crowd — slow burn, autonomous beats |

**Chat Mode is the new front door.** Story Mode is the labeled second option. The
autonomous engine is a *differentiator inside both modes*, not the whole product.

---

## The Problem

AI character chat apps are purely reactive: the story only moves when the user sends a
message. Close the app, and the narrative freezes. Meanwhile, text-only narrative apps
are high-friction and heady — a blank page, no face, no instant payoff. Users want a
character who feels alive, looks alive, and remembers them — without setup work.

---

## The Solution

- **Chat Mode** — Tap a character card from a visual discovery feed and you are chatting
  in seconds: first-person, in-character, with AI-generated illustrations that drop in at
  emotional beats, a memory that recalls who you are, and smart-reply suggestions. Zero
  setup. Sells the dream of *a beautiful character who likes you*.
- **Story Mode** — An AI-driven narrative engine where the story continues even when the
  user is offline. Characters act, scenes unfold, beats generate on a configurable
  cadence. The user steps in anytime to redirect — but is never obligated.
- **Shared moat** — In *both* modes, the autonomous engine can move things forward while
  the user is away. The companion can ping you ("Lyra texted 2h ago"); the story can
  advance on its own. No competitor does this.

---

## How It Works

### Chat Mode
1. **Discover** — Browse a visual feed of character cards by mood / genre / tag.
2. **Tap → chat** — Instant 1:1 first-person conversation (guest allowed, no account).
3. **See it** — Illustrations auto-drop at emotional beats; avatar art on every card.
4. **Be remembered** — Companion recalls your name, milestones, preferences across sessions.
5. **Low friction** — Smart-reply chips suggest responses; free type anytime.
6. **Come back** — Autonomous offline ping pulls you back ("a new message while you were away").
7. **Create** — Make a character from a prompt; avatar art is generated and published to the feed.

### Story Mode
1. **Start a story** — Pick/create a character, seed an opening scenario.
2. **Engine runs** — Autonomous engine generates beats on the chosen cadence, online or off.
3. **Step in anytime** — Read new beats; chat to add context, redirect, or speak to a character.
4. **Story absorbs input** — Each intervention shapes the next beats without overriding momentum.
5. **Repeat** — The loop runs indefinitely; the story is never finished, never frozen.

## Key Use Cases

- **Companion chat** — User taps a character and chats 1:1; the companion is visual,
  in-character, remembers them, and pings when away. (Chat Mode, primary.)
- **Character collecting** — User browses and switches between many companions like a gacha gallery.
- **Read-along mode** — User rarely chats; treats Story Mode as an evolving novel that writes itself.
- **Co-author mode** — User intervenes often, steering plot and dialogue while the engine fills gaps.
- **Scenario sandbox** — User drops "what if" prompts and watches the story metabolize them.

## Target Users

- AI companion / roleplay app users who want a visual, intimate, low-friction chat (Emochi crowd).
- Creative fiction enthusiasts who love ongoing, immersive narratives (Story Mode crowd).
- Fans of "living world" games and narrative-driven simulation.
- Casual readers and writers who enjoy AI storytelling with optional personal input.

## Core Features

### Chat Mode
1. **Discovery Feed** — Visual card gallery of characters, browsable by mood / genre / tag.
2. **Instant 1:1 Chat** — First-person, in-character dialogue; guest-friendly, no setup.
3. **AI Illustrations** — Avatar art on every character; inline scene art drops at emotional beats.
4. **Companion Memory** — Recalls user name, milestones, and preferences across sessions.
5. **Smart Replies** — Suggested user responses lower typing friction.
6. **Character Creation** — Build a character from a prompt; avatar art auto-generated and published.
7. **Offline Companion Pings** — Autonomous "while you were away" message (reuses the engine).

### Story Mode
8. **Autonomous Story Engine** — Advances the story at a configurable cadence (default 3 beats/day) without user input.
9. **AI Characters** — Customizable personalities, backstories, motivations, evolving arcs.
10. **Story Intervention** — Jump in via chat to add context, redirect, or speak to characters.
11. **Narrative Timeline** — Chronological log of AI beats + user interventions.
12. **Pacing Controls** — Slow (1/day), moderate (3/day), active (6+/day).
13. **Scenario Seeding** — Drop "what if" prompts that ripple into upcoming beats.

## Retention Mechanics (On-Brand "Stealth Gamification")

Reframe proven retention mechanics as **narrative / relationship artifacts**, preserving
the premium tone. They deliver the pull of gamification without arcade XP bars, points,
or leaderboards (overt forms remain Non-Goals).

1. **Offline pings / Offline beats (core loop)** — The autonomous engine moves things
   while the user is away; surface as "a new message / a new scene while you were gone."
   Strongest single retention lever — works in *both* modes, reuses existing engine.
2. **Companion Memory (relationship progression)** — Visible "she remembers…" ribbon.
   Deepening memory feels like a relationship growing, not a level bar.
3. **Story Continuity (streak, reframed)** — Surfaced as story/relationship *health*
   ("Lyra has been waiting 3 days"), not a flame counter.
4. **Story Depth / Chapters (progression, reframed)** — Stories mature into Acts and
   Chapters; a narrative milestone, not accumulated points.
5. **Memorable Moments (badges, reframed)** — Auto-curated highlight reel of standout
   beats; collectible feel, never an achievement popup.
6. **Today's Beat (daily reward, reframed)** — Free-tier 1-beat/day cadence doubles as a
   daily check-in hook, framed as the day's chapter, not a login bonus.

**Why allowed while gamification is a Non-Goal:** these expose the engine's existing
momentum and the relationship through narrative language. No XP, levels, streaks-as-counters,
or leaderboards.

---

## Non-Goals (Out of Scope)

- Overt gamified progression: visible XP bars, numeric levels, streak counters,
  leaderboards, achievement popups, daily quests. (On-brand reframings above are allowed.)
- Purely reactive chatbot with no autonomous progression.
- Multiplayer / shared-world stories (single-user at launch).
- **Voice and video** generation at MVP. (Note: **image generation is now IN scope** for
  Chat Mode avatars and inline illustrations — this reverses the earlier text-only stance.)
- Impersonal mass-market companion tone.

## Success Metrics

- **North Star:** Story Beats / Messages Generated (see below).
- **Engagement:** Active companions + active stories per user; return rate after offline
  pings/beats land; illustrations viewed per session.
- **Intervention rate:** Share of beats touched by user input (Story Mode co-author depth).
- **Retention:** D1 / D7 / D30 — does the living companion/story pull users back.
- **Conversion:** Free → paid uplift driven by illustration caps, offline pings, pacing.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Image cost:** Avatar + inline illustration = recurring image-gen spend. Mitigate via
  avatar caching, `illustrationFrequency` throttle, and `freeTierDailyImages` cap.
- **Quality drift:** Autonomous beats / long chats may wander or break character — needs
  memory + coherence guardrails.
- **LLM cost:** Continuous offline generation = recurring spend even when idle; cadence
  caps mitigate.
- **Relevance:** Beats/pings generated while offline may feel stale or ignore recent intent.
- **Safety/moderation:** Companion roleplay + autonomous generation + image gen raise
  content-safety surface area (text *and* visual).
- **Mode balance:** Risk of two half-products. Keep one shared backend; Chat Mode leads.
- **Unknowns:** Active user count, pricing, exact memory model, image-gen endpoint
  limits — all unconfirmed.

## Verified Operations (North Star)

**Primary operation:** Beat / Message Generated — a chat message, scene, chapter, or
narrative event produced by the engine or triggered by a user.
**Secondary operation:** User Intervention / Reply — a user message that shapes the
ongoing chat or story.
